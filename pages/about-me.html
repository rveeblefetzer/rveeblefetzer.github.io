About Me

I'm Rick Valenzuela, and welcome to yet another developer's blog.

It's mostly a playground to explore Pelican and a couple-few other things, so if you're here and this has actually been around for a bit, that's surprising.

I used to be a journalist, mostly working in Asia, but also in West Africa, Europe, Philadelphia and Little Rock. At one point, I taught security workshops at our foreign press club because I was already familiar with the concepts and tools, and there was a need and demand for it. This leads to explaining the blog title: Although my line of work relied on GUI-based applications, I was comfortable in my terminal. Covering conferences was made much easier folding sections in org-mode, and our newsroom never had to pay for software when authorities overstepped bounds and deleted photos. Teaching things led to learning others, and before long I was switching careers to developer.

My cameras still see love, but not as much as Python these days.

I'll probably be using this space to log helpful snippets and processes, as a better personal repository of thoughts than the scatterings currently in textfiles and physical notebooks. You'll likely find things in here about Python, vim, security, loving and stumbling my way through Arch Linux, and maybe the odd, misplaced photograph.

Thanks for even reading this page. Hit me up on Twitter or Mastodon if you want.
